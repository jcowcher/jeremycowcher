Article Suggestions
===================

The Promise of AI (Part 0) — Painting
---------------------------------------
Before AI, painting with a brush was code — it was the technology for turning
what's in your head into something others could see. Code was the next version
of that. Now AI is the next version of code. The throughline from brush to
keyboard to prompt: each generation gets a more direct path from imagination
to reality.

Loneliness as a Part of Greatness
----------------------------------
The preface to Van Gogh's autobiography starts with "Vincent Van Gogh was one
of the world's loneliest souls." Explore the relationship between isolation and
extraordinary work — the cost of seeing things differently before anyone else
does. Could tie into the builder/founder theme: building something new is
inherently lonely because you're working on something no one else sees yet.

Bill Simmons and Netflix
-------------------------
How Simmons built a media empire by being his own audience first, and what
Netflix's evolution says about betting on taste vs. data.

ESPN and the Need for Kayfabe
------------------------------
ESPN needs kayfabe — the professional wrestling tradition of maintaining the
illusion that everything is real. Sports media thrives on narratives, rivalries,
and storylines just like wrestling does. The difference is ESPN can't admit it.
What happens when the audience starts seeing through the performance? The NBA
is a case study — a league being dragged down by its media partner's need to
manufacture drama instead of letting the game speak for itself.

Elon Musk
----------
Love him or hate him — what his approach to building reveals about conviction,
speed, and ignoring consensus.

Deep Work
----------
Already drafted (see 20260303 Deep Work.rtf). Cal Newport's framework applied
to the AI era — when tools do the shallow work, deep work becomes the only
differentiator. Focus on the challenges: why deep work is so hard to actually
do. The constant pull of notifications, the discomfort of sustained focus, the
organizational pressure toward shallow busyness. Everyone agrees deep work
matters — almost nobody structures their life to protect it.

Zero to One vs. One to N
--------------------------
Thiel's distinction between creating something new (0 to 1) and copying what
works (1 to n). Most people and companies live in the 1-to-n world — scaling,
optimizing, iterating. The rare and valuable skill is 0 to 1: seeing something
that doesn't exist yet and willing it into being. What separates the two
mindsets, and why most advice is 1-to-n advice disguised as 0-to-1 thinking.

Making Something Mean Something Because You Say So
----------------------------------------------------
The power of declaration — deciding that something matters and acting
accordingly until the world agrees. Brands, movements, companies: they all
start as someone insisting this thing is real before anyone else believes it.
It's not delusion, it's the first act of creation. You define the meaning,
then you build the evidence.

The Great Men Theory
---------------------
Do individuals drive history, or do circumstances create the individual? What
this means for founders who believe they can bend the arc of their industry.
